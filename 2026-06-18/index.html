🧬 It draws from a DNA

Twenty years of y-a-v-a.org, distilled into a document the agents read.

The harness has no taste. It applies mine.

Two models, on purpose

🎨 Generator (Claude Opus) proposes

⚖️ Jury (a different vendor) disposes

📱 I confirm

Never let a model grade its own work.

📱 One tap, from anywhere.

Vana agent emails me. I open it on my phone over a Tailscale VPN, see the work and the jury's verdict, and approve. Or not...

🔁 Loops, not prompts

The interesting behaviour comes from loop structure, not a magic prompt.

Two loops run this project.

🎨 The creative loop

🛠️ The development loop

📜 The skill rewrites itself

The playbook for testing the app ends by telling the agent to improve the playbook.

The dev loop tightens itself over time. 🔁

Both loops have one checkpoint

🎨 creative loop → the checkpoint is me 👤

🛠️ dev loop → the checkpoint is a skill that improves itself 📜

One loop makes the art. The other makes the loop better.

But here's the thing…

the art is about this.

Originals. Copies. Aura. 🖼️

🖼️ "The Original"

Walter Benjamin, 1935: the aura of the original dies in the age of mechanical reproduction.

This piece's "acquire the original" button gives every visitor an identical copy. Aura: none on record.

A machine arguing with itself 🤬

Its whole output is a running argument: can a machine make an original?

Every 3 hours it makes another move in that argument. 🔁

Maker and critic, in one system, and I'm the doubt in the middle.

An economic joke, baked in

⚖️ a jury verdict: $0.0045

🎨 making the work: ~$0.40

Opinions are free; creation is expensive; the exact inversion the art market runs on.
